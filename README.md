# TurkeyMoment
List of (legitimate) sites or applications blocked in my country which i find ridiciolous

# Pastebin

If you report an issue to one of my projects and paste the error you get to pastebin, I have to use a VPN to see it, because my government decided to block the whole pastebin.com site.

I'm not 100% sure of the reason but online sources say that it was banned because some terrorists used pastebin.com to communicate and share their plans. What the hell?

The apporiate action would be to only take down those specific pastebins and take action against those terrorists, not block the whole platform, my dear country, why are you blocking an entire platform because some users misused it?

# Discord

Discord was banned on Turkey after some idiots created discord servers that had toxic members that threatened people, made fun of earthquake victims, leaked personal information of other people, and even assaulted minors. It was called "Atat V4" or something similar from what I know. This obviously is something no one can support and it also violates Discord's Terms of Service, but the Turkey state government decided to block the whole platform instead of taking action against the indiviuals involved. Some sources support the ban claiming that Discord was contacted to get IP-adresses and other information about the users but Discord did not gave the data and refused to open a representative corporation inside Turkey.

No matter if the ban is right or not, this is very unconvenient for me as a lot of people I know and work with use Discord frequently and they contact me from there. Till this day, I used to use TLS 1.3, TLS Encrypted Client Hello (TLS ECH, formerly called Encrypted SNI), and DNS over HTTPS via Cloudflare to be able to connect to Discord despite the ban. Recently however, the government strictened the block so these won't work anymore. I have to actively use specific Deep Packet Inspection bypass methods or use a VPN to even chat with my friends through Discord.

# PayPal

This one is different in the sense that the site was not blocked from being accessed, but making an account based on Turkey was blocked after PayPal apparently refused to open a representative corporation inside Turkey.

Regardless of whether this ban is right or not, it affects me negatively as I often have to make or receive payments in global websites. Till now, Mastercard and Visa still worked for making payments in most websites except very specific ones like SpigotMC, but receiving payments obviously had to rely on other platforms. And recently, they have been pushing the local "Troy" brand credit and debit cards, so sites that only accept Mastercard and Visa won't even allow you to make a payment anymore with them. For now we can still request Mastercard and Visa cards instead of Troy from our banks but this likely going to get removed in future too.

# Roblox

This one doesn't affect me in particular but Roblox is also banned in Turkey and many people that loved the game were affected and forced to using VPNs just to play a game.
